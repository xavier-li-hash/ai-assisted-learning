## 一、Summary Completion 解题流程

**特点：** 题目按原文段落顺序排列（顺序性强）

**解题步骤：**
1. 读 summary，理解整体意思
2. 找"锚点词"——summary 中不太可能被替换的专有名词/数字
3. 用锚点词定位原文段落
4. 从锚点前后找填空词，注意词性（名词？动词？形容词？）
5. 答案通常是原文的词，但 paraphrase 部分可能是同义词

**词性判断技巧：**

```
空格前是冠词（a/an/the）      → 名词
空格前是形容词                → 名词
空格前是助动词（can/was/is）  → 动词原形/过去分词
空格前是名词                  → 可能是动词（接宾语）或形容词（作补语）
```

## 二、练习材料

**原文：**

> The history of plastics is closely tied to the search for substitutes for natural
> materials. In the mid-19th century, billiard balls were typically made from ivory,
> which required the killing of elephants on a large scale. A competition was held to
> find an alternative material, and American inventor John Wesley Hyatt eventually
> developed celluloid, considered by many to be the first synthetic plastic.
>
> By the early 20th century, Leo Baekeland had created Bakelite, a material that could
> be moulded into virtually any shape and was both durable and heat-resistant. Unlike
> earlier plastics, Bakelite did not soften when reheated, making it ideal for electrical
> insulators, radio cases, and a wide range of industrial applications.
>
> The mass production of plastics accelerated dramatically after World War II, driven in
> part by advances in petrochemical technology. By the 1950s and 1960s, plastics had
> become ubiquitous in everyday life, replacing glass, metal, and wood in countless
> products. However, this widespread adoption also brought growing concerns about
> environmental impact, particularly the challenge of disposing of materials that could
> persist in the natural environment for hundreds of years.

---

**Summary（填写空格，每空1–3个词）：**

```
The development of plastics began as an attempt to find (1) ____alternative material_________ for
natural substances. Celluloid was first produced in the 19th century by (2) ___John Wesley Hyatt__________,
after a contest was organised to replace ivory, which had caused the (3) ___killing__________
of large numbers of elephants.

In the 20th century, Bakelite became significant because, unlike earlier materials,
it retained its (4) _____shape_____ when exposed to heat. It was therefore widely used
in (5) _____electrical insulators________ and industrial products.

After World War II, the (6) ____production_________ of plastics expanded rapidly thanks to
developments in petrochemical technology. Although plastics replaced many traditional
materials, concerns emerged about their (7) _____impact________ on the environment, since
they are difficult to (8) _____dispose________ and can remain in nature for centuries.
```

---

**答案（参考）：**

| 空格 | 答案 | 原文对应 |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | substitutes / alternatives | "search for substitutes for natural materials" |
| 2 | John Wesley Hyatt | "American inventor John Wesley Hyatt" |
| 3 | killing / death | "required the killing of elephants" |
| 4 | shape / form | "did not soften when reheated" → 保持形状 |
| 5 | electrical insulators / radio cases | "electrical insulators, radio cases" |
| 6 | production / use / adoption | "mass production … accelerated" |
| 7 | impact / effect | "environmental impact" |
| 8 | dispose of / break down | "disposing of materials" |

### 测试 C：近义词填空

用括号内的词替换划线部分，使句意不变：

1. Scientists **found** a new compound that reduces inflammation.
   （替换 found → _____ discovered______）[identified / revealed / discovered]

2. The results were **not definitive**, so further research is needed.
   （替换 → ______inconclusive_____）[inconclusive / ambiguous]

3. The policy **caused** widespread protests across the country.
   （替换 → ______led to_____）[led to / gave rise to / triggered]

4. Experts **support** stricter regulations on carbon emissions.
   （替换 → ____advocate for_______）[advocate for / endorse / champion]
