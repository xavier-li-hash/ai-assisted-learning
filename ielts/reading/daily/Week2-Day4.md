- 段落中的关键信息可能只有半句话，埋在长段中间
- 题目用的是 paraphrase，和原文词汇差距大
- 多个段落可能都提到同一主题，需要区分"谁说了什么"

## 二、信息匹配——隐晦信息定位技巧

### 核心技巧1：找"行为主体 + 动作"而不仅找名词

题目通常描述"某人/某物做了某事"。定位时不只找名词，要同时确认动词意义。

| 题目表述 | 原文可能的对应方式 |
|---------|-----------------|
| researchers discovered... | scientists found / identified / revealed... |
| governments failed to... | authorities did not / neglected to / were unable to... |
| the technology was initially opposed | early resistance to the method / critics argued against |
| higher costs led to... | the expense resulted in / financial burden caused... |

### 核心技巧2：警惕"相似段落"陷阱

当多个段落都讨论同一主题（如"污染"），用以下方式区分：
- **A段** 描述问题现状 → 题目问"描述了某现象"
- **B段** 分析原因 → 题目问"解释了某原因"
- **C段** 提出解决方案 → 题目问"建议了某方法"

**做法：** 确认段落的"功能角色"，不只是"主题匹配"。

### 核心技巧3：隐晦信息的4种藏法

```
1. 举例中藏信息    "...such as the case in Norway, where subsidies tripled"
                   → 题目问 "financial support increased significantly in one country"

2. 让步从句中藏信息  "Although widely accepted, the model has been challenged..."
                   → 题目问 "doubts have been raised about a commonly used approach"

3. 比较结构中藏信息  "Region A showed twice the rate of decline seen in Region B"
                   → 题目问 "one region experienced a more rapid decrease"

4. 否定句中藏信息   "The study found no evidence that temperature affected results"
                   → 题目问 "temperature was shown to have no impact"
```

**Passage（共5段，约700词）：**

**Paragraph A**
The problem of plastic waste in the world's oceans has grown significantly since the
widespread adoption of single-use plastics in the 1950s. Estimates suggest that over
eight million tonnes of plastic enter the ocean each year, yet this figure may
underrepresent the true scale of the issue, as much plastic sinks to the seafloor or
is consumed by marine organisms before it can be measured. Scientists studying the
phenomenon note that visibility of the problem depends heavily on where and how
measurements are taken, meaning that public awareness often lags behind the actual
severity of the situation.

**Paragraph B**
One of the most affected ecosystems is the coral reef. Coral reefs cover less than
one percent of the ocean floor but support approximately 25 percent of all marine
species. When plastic debris accumulates on reefs, it can block sunlight and reduce
the photosynthesis that reef-building corals depend on for survival. Additionally,
sharp-edged plastic fragments can physically damage coral tissue, creating wounds
that are vulnerable to bacterial infection. Research conducted in the Asia-Pacific
region found that the presence of plastic on reefs increased the likelihood of disease
in coral by 89 percent, a figure that alarmed marine biologists working in the field.

**Paragraph C**
Microplastics — fragments smaller than five millimetres — present a different and
arguably more insidious threat. Unlike large debris, which can sometimes be removed
through clean-up operations, microplastics are virtually impossible to extract from
the marine environment once dispersed. They are ingested by filter-feeding organisms
such as plankton and mussels, and from there enter the food chain. Studies have shown
that microplastics can interfere with the reproductive systems of certain fish species,
reducing fertility rates and threatening population stability. The chemical additives
used in plastic manufacturing — including flame retardants and plasticisers — are
released as the plastic degrades, adding a toxic dimension to what might otherwise
appear to be a purely physical problem.

**Paragraph D**
Efforts to address ocean plastic have taken several forms. International agreements,
such as those reached under the United Nations Environment Programme, have set
targets for reducing single-use plastic production, though critics argue that these
targets lack enforcement mechanisms and are therefore unlikely to produce meaningful
change within the required timeframe. On a smaller scale, coastal clean-up campaigns
have succeeded in removing large volumes of visible waste, but specialists caution
that such initiatives, while valuable for raising awareness, address only a fraction
of the total plastic burden in the ocean.

**Paragraph E**
A promising area of research involves the development of plastic-eating bacteria.
Scientists have identified several bacterial strains capable of breaking down certain
types of plastic, including PET, the material commonly used in drinks bottles. However,
the process is currently too slow and too limited in scope to represent a viable
large-scale solution. Some researchers advocate combining biological approaches with
advanced filtration technologies, suggesting that a hybrid strategy may prove more
effective than any single method. Others maintain that the most productive path
forward is not remediation but prevention — arguing that without dramatic reductions
in plastic production, any clean-up effort is ultimately futile.

## 五、题目组二：True / False / Not Given（强化版）

> 以下陈述与原文相比：
> - **TRUE** = 与原文信息一致
> - **FALSE** = 与原文信息矛盾
> - **NOT GIVEN** = 原文未提及

**判断规则提示：**
- NG 的特征：原文讲了 A，题目讲 B，A 和 B 不矛盾，但 B 根本没出现
- FALSE 的特征：原文明确给出与题目相反的事实（程度、方向、主体、数量）
- 不要用"常识"补充原文没说的内容 → 那是 NG

## 七、错题深度分析框架


做完后，对每道错题回答这3个问题：

```
Q1：我的定位词是什么？原文中对应的词/句是什么？
     → 找出 paraphrase 的落差

Q2：我为什么选错了段落/选了错误答案？
     → 是被表面词汇误导？还是忽略了动作主体？

Q3：这道题的隐晦信息用了上面哪种"藏法"？
```

### T/F/NG 答案与解析

| 题号 | 答案 | 解析 |
|-----|-----|------|
| 8 | FALSE | 原文说 "this figure may **underrepresent** the true scale"，即该数字低估了实际数量，与题目"accurately reflects"矛盾 |
| 9 | TRUE | 原文："less than one percent... support approximately 25 percent"，明确支持 |
| 10 | NOT GIVEN | 原文只说"Asia-Pacific region"，未说研究者来自欧洲；注意：研究地点 ≠ 研究者来源 |
| 11 | TRUE | 原文："Unlike large debris, which can sometimes be removed... microplastics are virtually impossible to extract" |
| 12 | NOT GIVEN | 原文提到化学添加剂"adding a toxic dimension"，但从未说它是"primary cause（主要原因）"——这是 NG 的典型陷阱：原文提到了，但没有说"最主要" |
| 13 | TRUE | 原文："critics argue that these targets lack enforcement mechanisms" = 缺乏执行机制 = 缺乏确保合规的手段 |
| 14 | FALSE | 原文说"currently too slow and too limited in scope to represent a viable large-scale solution"，并未商业化应用 |
| 15 | TRUE | 原文："the most productive path forward is not remediation but prevention" |

---

> **陷阱题重点复盘：**
>
> **题10（NG陷阱）：** "Asia-Pacific region"是研究地点，题目说研究者"based in Europe"——原文根本没说研究者在哪里，不能用"不在亚太"推出"在欧洲"。
>
> **题12（NG陷阱）：** 原文确实提到了化学添加剂有毒，但"primary cause"这个程度词是题目加上去的，原文从未做这个排名比较，所以是 NG 而不是 TRUE。这是最容易误判为 TRUE 的题型。
