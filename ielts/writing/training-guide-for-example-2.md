Band 8 Version

Introduction

The question of whether governments should prioritise public transport or road infrastructure has long been debated. While expanding road networks may bring short-term convenience for drivers, I would argue that investing in public transport is a more sustainable and effective solution in the long term.

⸻

Body 1（公共交通）

A key reason for prioritising public transport is its ability to reduce congestion at a systemic level. This is mainly because high-capacity systems, such as metros and buses, can transport large numbers of people while using significantly less road space per passenger. As a result, the overall volume of traffic is reduced rather than simply redistributed. In contrast, expanding roads often provides only temporary relief, as increased capacity tends to encourage more people to drive, eventually bringing congestion back to previous levels. For example, cities like Singapore and Tokyo have invested heavily in rail networks, enabling millions of people to commute efficiently each day. This not only keeps traffic under control but also reduces carbon emissions and improves urban air quality.

⸻

Body 2（道路）

Nevertheless, it would be unrealistic to assume that public transport alone can meet all transport needs. In many cases, road infrastructure remains essential for ensuring flexibility and accessibility. For instance, logistics companies depend on road networks to deliver goods efficiently, and families with young children may find private vehicles more practical for daily travel. Moreover, emergency services rely entirely on well-maintained roads to respond without delay. Therefore, a certain level of investment in road infrastructure is both necessary and justified, even if it should not take priority.

⸻

Conclusion

In conclusion, although both public transport and road infrastructure play important roles, they serve fundamentally different functions. However, given its ability to address congestion, environmental concerns, and social accessibility more effectively, public transport should remain the primary focus of government investment.
